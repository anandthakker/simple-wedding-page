How We Met

Jenn and Anand met in graduate school in 2006. At that point, becoming romantically involved was nowhere on our emotional radar. But, throughout our year in Cambridge, we caffeinated together early and often, watched movies when we should have been studying, pulled compensatory all-nighters, became enamored of the same professors, ate eggs at strange hours, debated trivial matters passionately, and browsed bookstores (as all annoying grad students do). Anand convinced Jenn to abandon previous plans so she could attend “Ugly Sweater Night” at a pub near his apartment. Jenn wrote one of Anand’s letters of recommendation for his first teaching job. Anand habitually stood in the frigid New England twilight, advising Jenn on seemingly pressing love conundrums. Jenn scolded Anand about the love conundrums that he didn’t even know he had.

In the years after grad school, we kept in touch, proffering late-night advice on dating and teaching, and taking weekend trips to Montreal, Washington, D.C., and San Diego. During that time, Anand worked at The Park School of Baltimore, where students are encouraged to call teachers by their first names and to speak their free minds to any passing stranger, while Jenn taught at a boarding school near Boston, providing grammatical instruction to well-coiffed young aspirers to the ruling class. Three years ago, in a fit of fury over something too boring to elucidate, Jenn left Milton Academy to work at Anand’s more progressive school, where we both currently teach. We’ve been roommates since June 2011, involved in top-secret “workplace fraternization” since December 2012, and engaged since December 2013.

Although we’ve been arguing with each other for over seven years, we still enjoy bickering over the best way to load the dishwasher, the ethics of the organic movement, how much cheese (both actual and aesthetic) one can reasonably consume without intervention, and whether certain movie characters should be abhorred for their shallowness or tolerated for their realism. Ever since we met, we’ve been accused of behaving like “an old married couple,” and we are excited to start fully inhabiting that old cliché, in all of its unpredictable vexations and delights.

Onward

Before embarking upon new career paths, putting down new roots, and settling into new routines, we will be traveling for four to six months. Anand will be doing some freelance work, Jenn will be doing some shameless mooching, and we’ll both be glimpsing things beyond the industrial skyline of Baltimore: the insides of foreign supermarkets, the embarrassingly superior public transit systems of European cities, the confounded expressions strangers wear when we butcher their native language, and – most importantly – how the same stars twinkle over other fields than these. Although not all trip details have been solidified, possible destinations include France, Spain, Kenya, India, Thailand, New Zealand, and Hawaii. In addition to seeing the usual sights and visiting every church known to humankind, we also hope to engross ourselves in books and music. Please let us know who your favorite artists and authors are, whether they’re little-known gems or guilty pleasures.

Registry

It’s become a threadbare platitude to say that your presence at our receptions will be better than any present you can give. But, really. We mean it! Feel free to make us a mix tape, divulge your famous chili recipe, enfold us in a full-body hug, or tip us off about which restaurants we should duck into while in Paris. Truly, we are not covetous of more “stuff” in our lives, especially since we’ll be packing all of our worldly possessions into storage for about a year.

We plan to make our travels somewhat economical by subletting apartments rather than staying in hotels and eating in rather than dining out. That being said, we are planning a few indulgences along the way. We’re told that it would be crazy to go to Kenya without touring a wildlife park, and we can’t imagine leaving the European continent without trekking to Florence to visit the statue of David.
